Introduction

Prompt to Prototype Vibe Coding Challenge

Welcome to the Prompt to Prototype Vibe Coding Challenge

About the Challenge

Prompt to Prototype is a fast-paced Artificial Intelligence (AI)-powered creation challenge. In one hour, you'll take an idea from a concept to a working web-based prototype using Copilot and GitHub Copilot.

You'll go through six key stages:

  1. Ideation â€” get or refine your idea
  2. Research â€” validate and explore your concept
  3. Product Requirements Document (PRD) â€” define your product requirements
  4. Branding â€” give your product a look, feel, and personality
  5. Prototype â€” build your Minimum Viable Product (MVP)

You'll finish with a clear, scoped idea and a functional MVP you can continue refining after the session.

Time Commitment: 60+ minutes.

Skill Level: Open to all levels and roles.

Learning Objectives / Outcomes

By completing this exercise, you will strengthen your ability to:

  • Generate and refine ideas using structured prompts and creative constraints
  • Conduct targeted research to explore a domain, validate an idea, and identify potential gaps
  • Perform a high-level market and competitive analysis using AI tools to synthesize insights quickly
  • Translate ideas into clear product requirements that guide a build
  • Develop a brand identity that communicates your product's vibe and value
  • Use AI as a creative and technical collaborator â€” from research and ideation to code generation and design
  • Scope an MVP effectively so you can deliver something functional within a short timeframe
  • Prototype rapidly with GitHub Copilot-assisted development
  • Package and present your work in a way that's compelling to share

What You'll Need

Before you begin, make sure you have:

  • Copilot Chat in the browser at https://m365.cloud.microsoft/chat/
    Available with your Microsoft 365 account. Use this for brainstorming and document creation.
  • GitHub Copilot in VS Code for building your prototype (Step 6)
    Download VS Code, then install the GitHub Copilot extension. Requires a GitHub Copilot subscription or free trial.
  • Screen Capture Software such as Snipping Tool (Windows), Screenshot (Mac), or browser extensions
    Useful for capturing inspiration images and sharing your prototype.

Expectations & Output

By the end of this session you should have:

  • A scoped, realistic MVP idea
  • Research summary (market insights, competitors, audience)
  • A basic PRD (must-have + nice-to-have features)
  • Brand identity (name, tagline, color palette, logo)
  • A working prototype (coded MVP)

Tips for Success

  • Scope small first â€” the goal is to finish something within the workshop time
  • Use stretch goals as optional extras â€” don't let them derail your MVP
  • Stay in the same Copilot chat for your project â€” this keeps all context in one place

Notes on Format

Sample Prompts and Responses

  • Each step has a sample prompt for you to copy and edit.
  • Prompts are written in a way to highlight specific techniques or functionality.
  • Prompts are prompt templates often requiring you to modify them - although they should pull the context of your conversation and might not require any modification.
