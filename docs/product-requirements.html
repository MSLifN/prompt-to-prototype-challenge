Objective/Outcomes

By completing this module, you will be able to:

  • Understand the essential sections of a Product Requirements Document (PRD)

4.1 Get Started

A PRD is the bridge between your idea and your build.

It answers:

  • What problem are you solving?
  • Who are you building for?
  • What exactly will you build first (MVP) vs later (stretch goals)?
  • How will you know if it‚Äôs working?

For this exercise, your PRD should be lightweight ‚Äî just enough to keep you focused while building. You may eventually use this document to ground the model when prototyping depending on your platform.

4.3 Review and Adjust

  • Check scope: MVP features should be achievable in the time limit
  • Simplify user stories: Keep them high-level so they're easy to translate into a build
  • Confirm success metrics: They should be measurable (even if simple)
  • Move extras to stretch goals so they don't distract from the core build

4.4 Save Your Product Requirements Document

Save your PRD so you can reference it when building your prototype.

Steps
  1. Once Copilot has generated your PRD, select all the text in the response (click and drag, or use Ctrl+A on Windows / Cmd+A on Mac)
  2. Copy the text (Ctrl+C on Windows / Cmd+C on Mac, or right-click > Copy)
  3. Open your preferred text editor or word processor where you saved your previous work (Word, Google Docs, Notepad/TextEdit, OneNote, Notion, etc.)
  4. Paste the PRD (Ctrl+V on Windows / Cmd+V on Mac) into your document
  5. If you requested a markdown file, save it as PRD.md or add it to your existing notes document
  6. Keep this PRD handy‚Äîit's your blueprint for the prototype build phase

Tip: Your PRD is the roadmap for your build. Reference the user stories and feature list directly when coding or designing your prototype.
