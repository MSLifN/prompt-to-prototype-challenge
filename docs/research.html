2.3 Use Copilot Chat for Research

Now you'll use Copilot Chat to research your project idea. The prompt below is designed to gather market insights, competitor analysis, and help you understand your target audience‚Äîall within the constraints of building a simple, beginner-friendly prototype.

Steps:
  1. Open Copilot Chat (copilot.microsoft.com or your preferred Copilot interface)
  2. You can continue in your existing conversation or start a new chat focused on research
  3. Copy the research prompt below by clicking the Copy button
  4. Before submitting, Edit the prompt and replace [INSERT PROJECT JSON] with the JSON structure you created in step 2.2
  5. Submit the modified prompt to Copilot
  6. Review the research results. Copilot will provide five tables covering trends, competitors, audience insights, differentiators, and design inspiration
  7. If you need more detail on any section, ask follow-up questions like "Can you expand on the competitor analysis?" or "What other differentiators could work for beginners?"
Prompt

SCOPE RULES (BEGINNER, LOW-COMPLEXITY):
- Keep ideas buildable as a single-page front-end (no auth, no external APIs, minimal state).

RESEARCH MODE:
- If browsing is available, use recent, credible sources. Prefer official docs, reputable media, or case studies.

TASKS (KEEP IT TIGHT):

   Columns: Claim | Why it matters | Source | Date (MM/YYYY)

2.4 Interpret and Refine

Once you get the research back:

  1. Highlight insights that surprise you ‚Äî these often lead to strong differentiators
  2. Note commonalities between competitors ‚Äî you may want to borrow successful patterns
  3. Refine your vibe or aesthetic words if better ones come up
  4. Update your MVP features if research suggests an easier or more impactful scope

2.5 Save Your Research Brief

Save your research output so you can reference it throughout the rest of the challenge.

Steps
  1. Once Copilot has provided your research results, select all the text in the response (you can click and drag, or use Ctrl+A on Windows / Cmd+A on Mac if clicking inside the response area)
  2. Copy the text (Ctrl+C on Windows / Cmd+C on Mac, or right-click > Copy)
  3. Open your preferred text editor or word processor:
    • Microsoft Word
    • Google Docs
    • Notepad (Windows) or TextEdit (Mac)
    • OneNote
    • Notion or similar note-taking apps
  4. Paste the research (Ctrl+V on Windows / Cmd+V on Mac) into your document and save it with a clear name like "Project Research - [Your Idea Name]"
  5. Keep this research handy‚Äîyou'll reference it in the next steps for branding and product requirements

Tip: Consider keeping all your workshop outputs in one document or folder so you can easily reference them as you progress through each step.
